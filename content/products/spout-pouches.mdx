## Overview

Spout Pouches combine the convenience of a rigid bottle with the efficiency of flexible packaging. A pre-inserted spout with cap is heat-sealed into the top of the pouch, allowing easy pouring, drinking, or dispensing of liquid and semi-liquid products.

This format has seen explosive growth across beverages, baby food, sauces, and even non-food categories like detergents and motor oil.

## Material Options

For spout pouches, material selection is critical because the pouch must withstand:

- **Drop impact** — Filled pouch must survive 1.2m drop test
- **Seal integrity** — Spout insertion area is the most critical seal point
- **Product compatibility** — Material must not react with contents

| Structure | Best For | Special Property |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| PET/AL/PE | Juices, sauces | High barrier, standard strength |
| PET/NY/PE | Detergents, chemicals | Puncture resistant |
| PET/AL/NY/PE | Retort products | Heat resistant, maximum strength |
| PET/AL/CPP | Hot-fill beverages | High temperature resistance |

## Retort-Capable Pouches

For products that require hot-filling or in-pouch sterilization (retort processing), we offer specialized high-temperature laminates that withstand 121°C for 30 minutes without delamination or seal failure.

## Quality Assurance

Every batch of spout pouches undergoes:

- **Drop test** — 1.2m free-fall with filled pouch
- **Leak test** — Vacuum leak detection on spout seal area
- **Torque test** — Cap removal torque within specification
- **Burst test** — Internal pressure resistance
