Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) is often the first thing brands ask about — and the answer varies more than most suppliers will admit. Understanding how MOQs work can save you money, prevent over-ordering, and help you choose the right packaging partner.

## What Is MOQ and Why Does It Exist?

MOQ exists because custom packaging manufacturing has fixed setup costs:

- **Cylinder/plate making** — Engraving your design onto printing cylinders costs the same whether you print 100 bags or 100,000.
- **Machine setup time** — Each production run requires cleaning, calibration, and material loading.
- **Raw material minimums** — Film and laminate suppliers have their own minimum roll sizes.

These fixed costs are amortized across the total quantity, which is why per-unit pricing drops significantly as quantities increase.

## Typical MOQs by Bag Type

| Bag Type | Typical MOQ | Best For |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Stand Up Pouch (digital print) | 500–1,000 | Startups, market testing |

## Digital vs. Gravure: The MOQ Game-Changer

### Digital Printing

Digital printing has transformed low-volume packaging. At SZZYPack, we offer digital printing for stand up pouches starting at **500 pieces**.

**Pros:**
- No cylinder/plate fees
- Faster setup (print-ready in hours, not days)
- Ideal for multiple SKUs with small quantities each

**Cons:**
- Higher per-unit cost at scale
- Slightly smaller color gamut vs. gravure
- Not available for all bag types

### Gravure Printing

Gravure is the industry standard for mid-to-large volume runs.

**Pros:**
- Lowest per-unit cost at scale (10,000+)
- Superior color consistency across runs
- Available for all bag types and materials

**Cons:**
- Cylinder costs ($200–500 per color)
- Higher MOQ requirement
- Longer setup time before first production

## How to Handle MOQ When You Are Small

### 1. Start with digital printing

If you are launching 2–3 SKUs and need 500–1,000 bags each, digital is your best option. Per-unit costs are higher, but you avoid cylinder fees and the risk of being stuck with 10,000 bags if the design changes.

### 2. Consolidate SKUs with the same material structure

If two products use the same film structure and dimensions but different artwork, ask your supplier if they can combine them into one production run. Some manufacturers (including SZZYPack) can do this, effectively splitting the MOQ across SKUs.

### 3. Negotiate a ramp-up schedule

Instead of ordering 5,000 units for your first run, propose:

- Run 1: 2,000 units (pay a small surcharge)
- Run 2: 5,000 units (standard pricing)
- Run 3: 10,000+ units (volume discount)

Many suppliers will accept this if they believe in the long-term relationship.

### 4. Stock bags with labels

If your quantity is very low (under 500), consider ordering blank stock bags and applying pressure-sensitive labels. This is not a long-term branding solution, but it can get you to market while you validate demand.
